01

Objective

The primary objective of this project is to conduct a comprehensive analysis of Snitch Fashion's sales data spanning from July 2023 to July 2025. The study aims to:

  • Examine revenue and profitability trends across product categories, cities, and customer segments.
  • Identify high-performing and underperforming product lines to support strategic decision-making.
  • Analyse the geographical distribution of sales to uncover regional market opportunities and gaps.
  • Compare B2B (Business-to-Business) and B2C (Business-to-Consumer) channel performance.
  • Study the impact of discounting strategies on final revenue and profit margins.
  • Provide data-driven recommendations to optimise sales performance, improve profitability, and guide marketing investments.

This analysis leverages 2,217 transaction records and 23 data fields to deliver actionable business intelligence.

02

Abstract

This report presents a structured data-driven analysis of Snitch Fashion Private Limited's sales operations across six major Indian cities: Mumbai, Ahmedabad, Hyderabad, Delhi, Bengaluru, and Pune. The dataset comprises 2,217 order records covering July 2023 to July 2025, encompassing 23 variables including product category, pricing, discounts, profit, segment classification, and geographical attributes.

Key Headline: Total revenue of ₹14.09 million with an aggregate profit of ₹2.20 million — representing an average profit margin of approximately 15.6%. The Dresses category led all segments at ₹2.54 million, while Mumbai topped cities with ₹2.51 million in revenue.

The study identifies significant year-on-year growth from 2023 to 2024, with revenue scaling from ₹1.01 million (partial year) to ₹6.51 million, stabilising at ₹6.57 million in 2025. Quarterly analysis highlights Q1 2025 as the peak performance period.

05

Conclusion

5.1 Key Takeaways

  • Strong Revenue Growth: Snitch Fashion scaled from ₹1.01 million (partial 2023) to over ₹6.5 million in both 2024 and 2025, signalling a maturing and scalable business model.
  • Category Leadership: The Dresses category consistently leads in both revenue (₹2.54M) and profit (₹402,703), making it a strategic priority for inventory investment.
  • Geographic Spread: Mumbai and Hyderabad together account for over 35% of total revenue, yet all six cities show relatively balanced performance.
  • B2B Dominance: With 66.5% of transactions from B2B clients, the brand has strong institutional relationships; B2C at 33.5% represents a high-value growth opportunity.
  • Discount Impact: Average discounts of ~0.65% directly affected net profitability. Certain records showed negative profit in Jackets and Shoes, highlighting the need for more targeted discounting policies.
  • Seasonal Patterns: Q3–Q4 2024 and Q1 2025 were peak periods, suggesting seasonal demand spikes that should be anticipated with pre-positioned inventory.

5.2 Strategic Recommendations

📈
Scale Dresses & Accessories
Increase inventory depth and marketing spend for top-performing categories. Hero products: Puffer Coat (₹714K), Wrap Dress (₹685K), Watches (₹672K).
🏙️
Revitalise Pune Market
Pune underperformed at ₹2.05M. Targeted city campaigns, local influencer partnerships, and promotional events can unlock latent demand.
🛍️
B2C Growth Strategy
Invest in digital marketing, loyalty programmes, and personalised shopping experiences to shift towards the higher-margin B2C segment.
💰
Rationalise Discount Policy
Implement category-specific discount caps and profit floor thresholds to prevent margin erosion. Negative-profit orders in Jackets warrant immediate pricing review.
📦
Seasonal Inventory Planning
Leverage quarterly trend data to build demand-forecasting models, ensuring adequate stock during peak quarters (Q3–Q4) and reducing overstock in off-peak periods.
🤝
Segment-Based Pricing
Develop distinct pricing tiers for B2B and B2C channels to maximise margins in each segment without cannibalising institutional relationships.
Final Note: Snitch Fashion is well-positioned in the Indian fashion market with strong fundamentals. A focused strategy targeting category strengths, geographic expansion, and channel diversification — supported by disciplined pricing analytics — will be key to sustaining and accelerating growth in the coming years.
